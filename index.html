SHORT BIO

I am a second-year Ph.D. student in Dynamical Systems and Ergodic Theory at the Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro, under the guidance of Lorenzo J. Díaz.

Previously, I completed my Master's degree at PUC-Rio under the supervision of Lorenzo J. Díaz and the co-supervision of Pablo G. Barrientos. My Master's thesis, entitled "Statistical behavior of skew products: schwarzian derivative and arc-sine laws", explored the predictable and historical behavior of skew product maps. Furthermore, it established a connection between the historical behavior of these systems and the arcsine law.

RESEARCH

My research focuses on the statistical properties of dynamical systems, with an emphasis on physical measures and heterodimensional cycles. Currently, I am studying the mixing properties of skew translation maps and examining heteroclinic tangencies associated with hyperbolic measures. .
